Experiments / R&D

The work is still being tested, refined, and questioned.

This page exists to show that the project is still in motion. We are testing ideas, trying to understand tradeoffs, and documenting what the process is teaching us rather than pretending everything is already solved.

What We Are Testing

Broadly, we are exploring energy behavior, chamber ideas, interface assumptions, practical cooking use cases, and how much transparency or feedback is actually helpful to users.

What We Are Learning

Some insights strengthen the concept. Others reveal new constraints, safety questions, or design challenges that need more thought. Both outcomes are useful.

What We Want To Improve Next

We want sharper prototypes, better comparisons between ideas, and clearer decisions about which technical directions are worth pursuing further.

Research Themes

The main questions currently shaping the work.

These are framed as working themes, not closed answers.

Energy distribution

How can microwave energy be understood and managed more intentionally within the cooking space?

User trust

What information or feedback would actually help the experience feel clearer rather than more complicated?

Hardware practicality

Which ideas remain realistic when materials, shielding, safety, manufacturing, and cost are considered together?

Use-case fit

Where does this concept help most, and where might existing appliances already be good enough?

Transparency Matters

Honest R&D builds more trust than polished overstatement.

We would rather show the project as it really is: promising in some areas, uncertain in others, and still very much under active development.

Next Improvement Areas

Prototype depth

Move from broad concept framing toward more specific build-and-test cycles.

Measurement

Improve the quality of comparisons and observations across experiments.

Decision quality

Use what we learn to narrow the roadmap more confidently.
